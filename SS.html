  • Resources for all social studies subjects:
    • Biography.com: This website provides biographies about historical persons.
    • Library of Congress: This website provides a wide range of historical images, records, documents, artifacts, newspapers, manuscripts.
    • Sparknotes: This site This site provides information about events in European and American history.

  • US History and Government
    • United States Holocaust Memorial Museum:
      This site provides information about the Holocaust, including a detailed timeline of events, articles, and maps.
    • Documents for the Study of American History:
      This site provides a list of mostly primary documents, ordered chronologically from 800 AD to 2005, for the study of American history.
    • IPL2:
      This website gives a list of US presidents, with their birthdays, government positions, cabinet members during their presidency, notable events, and more internet sources about each president.
    • Sparknotes:
      This site gives information about the branches, structure and the policies of the United States government.

  • European History
    • Euro Docs:
      This website gives primary historical resources from each European Union country.
    • European Union:
      This website gives information about each of the countries in the European Union, including official language, capital, currency, government, population, etc.

  • World History
    • WWI Document Archive:
      This archive contains primary and secondary sources written during the WWI period.
    • WWII Documents:
      This site provides a range of documents, correspondances, agreements between nations, and armistices from the WWII period.

  • Geography
    • CIA World Book:
      This site provides comprehensive information about each nation, including geography, government, economy, people and society, military and safety, etc.
