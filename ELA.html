Resources for all English studies:
  • Sparknotes:
    This site provides summaries, quizzes, study questions, in-depth exploration of themes and motifs for literature, including Shakespeare plays.
  • Cliffsnotes:
    This site provides book summaries by acts/chapters, character analysis, quizzes, and study help.
  • Shmoop:
    This site provides book summaries, exploration into the themes, quizzes, character analysis, and even a glossary of literary terms.
  • Grade Saver:
    This site provides study guides for books of literature as well as poems.
  • Book Rags:
    This site provides some free book notes that contain chapter by chapter summary, quote analysis, as well as student study packs that are available for download.
  • Study Blue:
    This site provides a list of 166 literary terms for students to study from.
  • Depaul University:
    This site explains the basic structures and characters of comedy and tragedy plays.
  • Owlcation:
    This is an article pointing out the main structures of Shakespeare's plays by genre: history, comedy, tragedy. This one explains the 4 different types of writing styles.
  • Venngage:
    This article breaks down the steps in the Hero's Journey, also called the monomyth. Contains movie examples!
  • Mental Floss:
    This article explains 21 rhetorical devices for students who are writing persuative speeches or preparing for debates.
  • Fast Web:
    site gives tips for students writing essays.
  • Poetry Foudation:
    This provides a list of the different types of poems as well as poetic terms. In Poetry Foundation students can also find collections of poems and discover new poets.
